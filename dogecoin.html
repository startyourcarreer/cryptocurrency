History of DOGE

Dogecoin was created by software engineers Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer in December 2013. The idea behind Dogecoin was to create a fun and friendly cryptocurrency that could reach a broader audience than Bitcoin. Inspired by the popular "Doge" meme featuring a Shiba Inu dog, Markus and Palmer aimed to satirize the hype surrounding cryptocurrencies at the time. Dogecoin was officially launched on December 6, 2013. Within the first month, the Dogecoin website had over a million visitors, and the coin quickly developed a dedicated online community. Unlike Bitcoin, which has a limited supply, Dogecoin has an unlimited supply, with billions of coins in circulation. Dogecoin is based on the Litecoin blockchain and uses the Scrypt algorithm for its proof-of-work consensus mechanism. This allows for faster block times and lower transaction fees compared to Bitcoin.

Market Supply of DOGE

Unlike many other cryptocurrencies, Dogecoin does not have a fixed maximum supply. This means that there is no cap on the number of Dogecoins that can be mined, leading to a continuously increasing supply. Dogecoin's market capitalization is approximately $15.41 billion. This metric reflects the total market value of all circulating DOGE tokens. The market supply of Dogecoin is characterized by its large and continuously increasing circulating supply, driven by its uncapped maximum supply and fixed block rewards.
