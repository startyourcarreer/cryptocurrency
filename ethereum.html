History of Ethereum

Ethereum was initially described in late 2013 in a white paper by Vitalik Buterin, a programmer and co-founder of Bitcoin Magazine. The development work began in early 2014 and was crowdfunded later that year, with the network going live on July 30, 2015. Ethereum allows anyone to deploy permanent and immutable decentralized applications onto it, with which users can interact. It has undergone several significant upgrades, including the transition from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake in September 2022.

Market Supply of Ethereum

As of now, Ethereum has a circulating supply of approximately 120.27 million ETH and a market capitalization of around $326.48 billion. The total supply of Ether is influenced by various factors, including mining rewards, staking rewards, and burnt fees.
